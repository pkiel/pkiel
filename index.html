About me

Name: Patrick Kiel

Email:

Degree: Marine Science/Biology (B.S.)

Current: NOAA AOML Coral Program

I am passionate about coral reef ecosystems and the value they bring to coastal communities. As a coral reef ecologist, I am interested in collaborating with expert biologists, engineers, and computer scientists to leverage novel research techniques to conserve and restore coral reef ecosystems. My career is guided by my vision to ensure the beauty, economic and ecological significance, and scientific curiosity of coral reefs are ensured for generations to come.

I am a recent graduate of the University of Miami with a degree in Marine Science and Biology currently working as an intern in NOAAâ€™s AOML Coral Program. I am broadly interested in coral reef ecology with prior research expierence in conserving coral ecosystems in marginal environments and understanding how the persistance of habitat structure drives marine eosystems. I seek to understand the ecosystem services and ecological functions derived from reef structure and how these services and functions are changing alongside the global decline of coral ecosystems. Currently, I am working on understanding the role of coral reef restoration in recovering complex reef habitat and bolstering coastal resilience. My research interests span mesophotic reefs, ecosystem services, and persistence of habitat structure. My formal CV can be found here.

My work combines research across the field and lab to understand how global climate change and human use affect marine ecosystems near urban epicenters. Please explore this site to learn more about my work.
